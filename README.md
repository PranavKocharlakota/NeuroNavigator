**NeuroNavigator** 🧠

This project implements an intelligent clinical trial discovery platform that helps identify brain cancer clinical trials that best match a patient’s medical profile.

Instead of manually searching through complex eligibility criteria on ClinicalTrials.gov, NeuroNavigator analyzes tumor characteristics, treatment history, and performance status to highlight trials a patient may qualify for and explain why they are relevant in plain language.

The platform provides a clean interface for entering clinical information, viewing ranked trials, and exploring trial locations on an interactive map.

___________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

**Features / Highlights**

Patient profile form for entering tumor and treatment information

Clinical trial matching based on diagnosis, molecular markers, and treatment history

Ranked trials with visual match scores

Plain-language explanations describing why a trial may be relevant

Expandable trial cards showing key eligibility details

Interactive map displaying trial site locations using Google Maps

**How It Works**

The user enters their tumor profile and treatment history.

The system queries active clinical trials.

Trials are filtered and ranked based on likely eligibility.

Results are displayed with match scores and explanations.

Users can explore trial locations on an interactive map.

**Disclaimer**

This tool is intended to support discussions with healthcare professionals and should not be used as a substitute for medical advice.
Clinical trial eligibility must always be confirmed with physicians and official trial coordinators.
